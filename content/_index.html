Your mission

Within past 4 days 2 different hospitals in a small town (Smallville, Indiana) reported 23 people visiting emergency rooms complaining about fever, fatigue, coughing and difficulty breathing. Two people have died soon after being hospitalized, several are still at the hospital. Local Public Health Departments were called to investigate if there is an outbreak and to put an end to it if it is an actual outbreak. The notification was also sent to the CDC and the mayor of the town was notified.

Next steps:

Step 2:

  • Do your research.
  • Explore potential reasons for these symptoms.
  • Identify the level of emergency (if it is an outbreak) based on the severity and timing.
Ask for additional information if needed.

Step 3:

  • Establish if these symptoms are caused by an infectious pathogen. Ask for more information if needed.
  • Establish if these individuals are a part of an outbreak. If yes, what is the most likely route of transmission?
  • Identify if there is a potential for more people being infected. If yes, how much more?

Step 4:

  • Order lab tests. Define the sets of pathogens to test for. Some of the tests may take longer to process.
